# Privacy policy wording

The generated privacy policy should use a consistent, reader-friendly style.

Use direct address (`you`, German `Sie`) where the reader is the person being addressed. This applies especially to:

- data subject rights, such as access, correction, deletion, objection, and complaints;
- account-related information for registered users;
- browser, cookie, tracking, and device settings;
- contact requests or requests to report visibility problems.

Use neutral wording where the reader is not necessarily the person described by the data. This applies especially to:

- published genealogical data about living or deceased third parties;
- data that may relate to another person in a family tree;
- general descriptions of data categories, safeguards, retention, and visibility rules.

The goal is not to avoid direct address everywhere. The goal is to avoid misleading direct address. Text aimed at the website visitor should remain clear and accessible, while text about genealogical data should not imply that every described person is also the current reader.

Examples:

- Reader-facing: "You have the right to request correction of data concerning you."
- Registered users: "As a registered user, you can view, correct, or delete your account data yourself."
- Genealogical third-party data: "Personal data published on this website, such as names, e-mail addresses, photos, addresses, or life dates, is used only for family history and genealogical research."
- Visibility requests: "If, for specific reasons, data about a person should not be visible even within the group of authorized registered users, please inform me/us."
